Crochet stitches are the building blocks of crochet fabric, created by pulling loops of yarn through other loops using a crochet hook. Basic stitches include the chain, single crochet, half double crochet, and double crochet, each varying in height and texture. Combining different stitches creates patterns, textures, and shapes in crochet projects. This page referres to US standard metrics (as opposed to UK, which varies slightly).

Slip Stitch

Slip stitch, abbreviated as ss or sl st in some patterns, is the shortest crochet stitch and is often used to join rounds or move across stitches without adding height.

Single Crochet

Single crochet, or sc in patterns, is a tight, compact stitch that creates a dense fabric, commonly used in items like amigurumi.

Half Double Crochet

Half double crochet, known as hdc in many patterns, is slightly taller than single crochet and offers a good balance of height and flexibility

Double Crochet

Double crochet, or dc in some patterns, is one of the tallest basic stitches, creating an open, airy texture that works well for blankets, garments, and lacy designs.

Here's a helpful YouTube tutorial.

Treble Crochet

Treble crochet, abbreiviated as tr in most patterns, is a tall stitch created by wrapping the yard around your hook twice before inserting it into the stitch. It produces a very open, airy texture and adds significant height to a row. While the four above stithes are the most common, some more advanced patterns may ask for treble crochet stitches. Stitches even taller than treble, like double treble (dtr) or triple treble (trtr), follow the same basic process just with more yarn overs, allowing increasing heights.
