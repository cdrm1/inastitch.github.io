A crochet hook is a handheld tool with a curved tip used to pull yarn through loops to create crochet stitches. They come in various sizes and materials like aluminum, plastic, or wood, depending on the yarn and project. The hook size affects the tightness and texture of the finished fabric.

Inline Hooks

Inline crochet hooks have a straight shaft with the hook tip aligned evenly with the handle, forming a consistent, uniform shape. This design helps maintain even tension and can make stitches more consistent. Many crocheters find inline hooks easier for beginners or for making precise stitches.

Tapered Hooks

Tapered crochet hooks have a rounded, narrower throat that gradually tapers down to the hook tip, which is slightly offset from the handle. This design allows for smoother, quicker movement and is often preferred for a more relaxed crocheting style. Tapered hooks are popular among crocheters who like a fluid, less rigid stitching experience.

Handled Hooks

Handled crochet hooks feature a thicker or ergonomic grip attached to the shaft, designed to reduce hand strain and improve comfort during long crocheting sessions. They come in various shapes and materials like rubber or silicone. Handled hooks are ideal for those with arthritis or anyone looking for better control and less fatigue.
